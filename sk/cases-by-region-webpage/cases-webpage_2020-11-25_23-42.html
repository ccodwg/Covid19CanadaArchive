Where an official translation is not available, Google™ Translate can be used. Google™ Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 25th, there are 164 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, 164 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 7,047 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (7), Far North Central (3), Far North East (8), North West (1), North Central (10), North East (5), Saskatoon (37), Central East (3), Regina (69), South West (9), South Central (1) and South East (9) zones and two (2) new cases have pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending resident information (from Nov 20) was assigned to South Central (1)
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 7,047 cases are confirmed
    • 2,042 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 1,478 cases are from the north area (512 north west, 690 north central, 276 north east)
    • 1,164 cases are from the Regina area
    • 951 cases are from the south area (349 south west, 371 south central, 231 south east)
    • 815 cases are from the far north area (554 far north west, 75 far north central, 186 far north east)
    • 566 cases are from the central area (258 central west, 308 central east)
    • 31 cases have pending residence information
  • 3,012 cases out of the 7,047 cases are considered active (42.7%)
  • 1,494 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 7% (460) of cases are travel-related, 45% (3,195) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 21% (1,473) have no known exposures and 27% (1,919) are under investigation by local public
  • 216 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 111 individuals are hospitalized; including 92 inpatient hospitalizations and 19 ICU hospitalizations
  • 37 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.5%
  • A total of 3,998 individuals have recovered (56.7%). There are 79 new recoveries located in the Far North West (7), Far North Central (3), North West (4), North West (12), North Central (6), North East (5), Saskatoon (24), Central East (3), South West (7), South Central (7) and South East (1) zones
  • 326,871 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 23, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan’s per capita rate was 211,426 people tested per million population.  The national rate was 293,363 people tested per million population.
Released: November 25, 2020
