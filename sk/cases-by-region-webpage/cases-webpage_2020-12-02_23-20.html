Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of December 2nd, there are 238 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, bringing the total to 8,982 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (6), Far North Central (3), Far North East (16), North West (17), North Central (25), North East (3), Saskatoon (109), Central East (4), Regina (36), South West (8), South Central (1) and South East (3) zones and seven (7) new cases have pending residence information
  • One (1) case assigned to Saskatoon (from Nov 27) was removed from the counts
  • Five (5) cases with pending residence information were assigned to the Far North East (from Nov 30 (1)), North Central (Nov 26 (1)), Regina (Nov 30 (2)) and South East (Nov 30 (1)) zones
  • 8,982 cases are confirmed
    • 2,752 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 1,809 cases are from the north area (652 north west, 847 north central, 310 north east)
    • 1,634 cases are from the Regina area
    • 1,151 cases are from the south area (442 south west, 412 south central, 297 south east)
    • 966 cases are from the far north area (618 far north west, 83 far north central, 265 far north east)
    • 637 cases are from the central area (275 central west, 362 central east)
    • 33 cases have pending residence information
  • 3,970 cases out of the 8,982 cases are considered active (44.2%)
  • 1,862 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 50% of the cases male and 50% female
  • 5% (480) of cases are travel-related, 42% (3,812) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 24% (2,144) have no known exposures and 28% (2,546) are under investigation by local public health
  • 268 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • As of December 2nd, a total of 132 individuals are hospitalized; including 106 inpatient hospitalizations and 26 ICU hospitalizations
  • 53 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.6%
  • A total of 4,959 individuals have recovered (55.2%). There are 84 new recoveries located in the North West (10), North Central (5), North East (1), Saskatoon (35), Central West (5), Central East (2), South West (3), South Central (13) and South East (10) zones
  • 350,391 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 30, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan's per capita rate was 223,304 people tested per million population. The national rate was 307,889 people tested per million population.
Released: December 2, 2020
