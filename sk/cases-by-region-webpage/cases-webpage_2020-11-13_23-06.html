Highlights

  • As of November 13th, there are 81 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 – 81 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province – bringing the total to 4,513 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (1), Far North Central (6), Far North East (3), North West (7), North Central (10), North East (11), Saskatoon (5), Central East (4), Regina (16), South West (6), South Central (8) and South East (4) zones
  • Two (2) case with pending residence information from November 11 was assigned to the North West (2) zone
  • One (1) case from Oct 16 from North West was assigned to pending for further investigation
  • One (1) case from Nov 11 from North West was deemed to be a duplicate and removed
  • Four (4) cases with pending residence information (Nov 8, Nov 9 and Nov 11) were deemed non-SK residents and removed
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 4,513 cases are confirmed
    • 1,237 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 948 cases are from the north area (309 north west, 456 north central, 183 north east)
    • 683 cases are from the Regina area
    • 611 cases are from the south area (253 south west, 256 south central, 102 south east)
    • 564 cases are from the far north area (453 far north west, 23 far north central, 88 far north east)
    • 459 cases are from the central area (203 central west, 256 central east)
    • 11 cases have pending residence information
  • 1,427 cases out of the 4,513 cases are considered active (31.6%)
  • 915 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 9% (401) of cases are travel-related, 46% (2,071) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 23% (1,018) have no known exposures and 23% (1,023) are under investigation by local public health
  • 134 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 53 individuals are hospitalized; including 38 inpatient hospitalizations and 15 ICU hospitalizations
  • 29 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.6%
  • A total of 3,057 individuals have recovered (67.7%). There are 108 new recoveries located in the Far North West (2), Far North East (1), North West (2), North Central (6), Saskatoon (53), Central East (2), Regina (27), South West (2), South Central (1) and South East (12) zones
  • 291,072 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 11, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan’s per capita rate was 193,007 people tested per million.  The national rate of 270,258 people tested per million.
Released: November 13, 2020
