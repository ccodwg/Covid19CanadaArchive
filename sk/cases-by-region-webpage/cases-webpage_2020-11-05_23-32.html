Highlights

  • As of November 5th, there are 129 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 – 129 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province – bringing the total to 3,536 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (6), Far North East (1), North West (10), North Central (30), Saskatoon (55), Central West (1), Central East (4), Regina (13), South West (1), South Central (2) and South East (1) zones and five (5) new cases have pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending residence information from November 3 was deemed to be an out of province resident and removed from the counts
  • There were no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,536 cases are confirmed
    • 942 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 755 cases are from the north area (232 north west, 380 north central, 143 north east)
    • 508 cases are from the south area (231 south west, 217 south central, 60 south east)
    • 466 cases are from the Regina area
    • 456 cases are from the far north area (400 far north west, 0 far north central, 56 far north east)
    • 402 cases are from the central area (198 central west, 204 central east)
    • Seven (7) cases have pending residence information
  • 877 cases out of the 3,536 cases are considered active (24.8%)
  • 720 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases are males and 51% are females
  • 11% (380) of cases are travel-related, 48% (1,690) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 24% (847) have no known exposures and 18% (619) are under investigation by local public health
  • 108 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 32 individuals are hospitalized; including 27 inpatient hospitalizations and five (5) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,634 individuals have recovered (74.5%). There are 50 new recoveries located in the Far North West (2), Far North East (1), North West (6), North Central (2), North East (8), Saskatoon (21), Central West (2), Central East (3), Regina (3), South West (1) and South East (1) zones.
Released: November 5, 2020
