Where an official translation is not available, Google™ Translate can be used. Google™ Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 10th, there are 127 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, 125 new cases in Saskatchewan and two (2) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 4,214 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (6), Far North Central (4), Far North East (6), North West (11), North Central (2), North East (2), Saskatoon (50), Central West (2), Central East (6), Regina (31) and South Central (6) zones and one (1) new case has pending residence information
  • Three (3) cases with pending residence information from November 8 were assigned to the North Central (3) zone
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 4,214 cases are confirmed
    • 1,179 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 902 cases are from the north area (294 north west, 436 north central, 172 north east)
    • 604 cases are from the Regina area
    • 553 cases are from the south area (243 south west, 232 south central, 78 south east)
    • 520 cases are from the far north area (440 far north west, 9 far north central, 71 far north east)
    • 445 cases are from the central area (203 central west, 242 central east)
    • 11 cases have pending residence information
  • 1,305 cases out of the 4,214 cases are considered active (31.0%)
  • 854 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults,  49% of the cases are males and 51% are females
  • 9% (394) of cases are travel-related, 46% (1,931) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 22% (914) have no known exposures and 23% (975) are under investigation by local public health
  • 122 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 44 individuals are hospitalized; including 33 inpatient hospitalizations and 11 ICU hospitalizations
  • 29 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,880 individuals have recovered (68.3%). There are 111 new recoveries located in the North West (3), North Central (5), North East (3), Saskatoon (71), Central East (3), Regina (25) and South Central (1) zones.
  • 285,858 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 8, 2020, when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan's per capita rate was 190,095 people tested per million population and was lower than the national rate of 265,663 people tested per million population.
Released: November 10, 2020
