Where an official translation is not available, Google™ Translate can be used. Google™ Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Highlights

  • As of October 30th, there are 76 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 – 76 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province – bringing the total to 3,066 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (2), North West (2), North Central (19), North East (4), Saskatoon (34), Central East (4), Regina (7), South Central (2) and South East (2) zones
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,066 cases are confirmed
    • 761 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 608 cases are from the north area (190 north west, 287 north central, 131 north east)
    • 500 cases are from the south area (229 south west, 215 south central, 56 south east)
    • 438 cases are from the far north area (384 far north west, 0 far north central, 54 far north east)
    • 380 cases are from the central area (196 central west, 184 central east)
    • 377 cases are from the Regina area
    • Two (2) cases have pending residence location
  • 742 cases out of the 3,066 cases are considered active (24.2%)
  • 611 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 12% (362) of cases are travel-related, 49% (1,514) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 25% (781) have no known exposures and 13% (409) are under investigation by local public health
  • 98 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 22 individuals are hospitalized; including 16 inpatient hospitalizations and six (6) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.8%
  • A total of 2,299 individuals have recovered (75.0%). There are 41 new recoveries located in the Far North West (4), Far North East (2), North West (5), North Central (10), North East (1), Central East (4), Regina (12) and South Central (2) zones and one (1) case pending location.
Released: October 30, 2020
