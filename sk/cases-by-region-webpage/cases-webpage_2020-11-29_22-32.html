Highlights

  • As of November 29th, there are 351 new confirmed cases of COVID‐19, bringing the total to 8,239 reported cases.
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (10), Far North Central (1), Far North East (8), North West (28), North Central (10), North East (12), Saskatoon (94), Central West (2), Central East (15), Regina (120), South West (19), South Central (10) and South East (18) zones and four (4) new cases have pending residence information.
  • 8,239 cases are confirmed
    • 2,478 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 1,681 cases are from the north area (599 north west, 785 north central, 297 north east)
    • 1,466 cases are from the Regina area
    • 1,079 cases are from the south area (407 south west, 399 south central, 273 south east)
    • 889 cases are from the far north area (591 far north west, 80 far north central, 218 far north east)
    • 616 cases are from the central area (271 central west, 345 central east)
    • 30 cases have pending residence information
  • 3,605 cases out of the 8,239 cases are considered active (43.8%).
  • 1,710 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 50% of the cases male and 50% female.
  • 6% (470) of cases are travel‐related, 43% (3,562) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 21% (1,768) have no known exposures and 30% (2,439) are under investigation by local public health.
  • 252 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related.
  • As of November 29th, a total of 115 individuals are hospitalized; including 92 inpatient hospitalizations and 23 ICU hospitalizations.
  • 45 Saskatchewan residents with COVID‐19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.5%
  • A total of 4,589 individuals have recovered (55.7%). There are 68 new recoveries located in the Far North West (5), Far North Central (1), Far North East (5), North West (5), North Central (3), North East (3), Saskatoon (6), Central West (1), Regina (23), South West (6), South Central (4) and South East (6) zones.
  • 341,236 COVID‐19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan.
  • As of November 27, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan’s per capita rate was 218,118 people tested per million population. The national rate was 301,813 people tested per million population.
Released: November 29, 2020
