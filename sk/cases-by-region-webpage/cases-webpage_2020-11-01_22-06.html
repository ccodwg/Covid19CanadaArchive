Where an official translation is not available, Google™ Translate can be used. Google™ Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 1st, there are 74 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 – 74 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province – bringing the total to 3,218 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (5), North West (5), North Central (12), North East (1), Saskatoon (25), Central East (2), Regina (19), South West (1) and South East (3) zones and one (1) case has pending residence location
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,218 cases are confirmed
    • 827 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 642 cases are from the north area (198 north west, 306 north central, 138 north east)
    • 504 cases are from the south area (230 south west, 215 south central, 59 south east)
    • 445 cases are from the far north area (391 far north west, 0 far north central, 54 far north east)
    • 414 cases are from the Regina area
    • 383 cases are from the central area (196 central west, 187 central east)
    • Three (3) cases have pending residence location
  • 798 cases out of the 3,218 cases are considered active (24.8%)
  • 643 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 12% (371) of cases are travel-related, 49% (1,577) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s),
  • 25% (801) have no known exposures and 15% (469) are under investigation by local public health
  • 101 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 33 individuals are hospitalized; including 26 inpatient hospitalizations and seven (7) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.8%
  • A total of 2,395 individuals have recovered (74.4%). There are 15 new recoveries located in the North West (1), North Central (6), Saskatoon (2), Central East (2), Regina (3) and South East (1) zones.
Released: November 1, 2020
