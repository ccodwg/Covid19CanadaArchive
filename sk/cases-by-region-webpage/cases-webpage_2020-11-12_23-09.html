Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 12th, there are 111 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, 111 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 4,437 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (5), Far North Central (8), Far North East (6), North West (7), North Central (8), Saskatoon (24), Central East (7), Regina (25), South West (4), South Central (5) and South East (7) zones and five (5) new case have pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending residence information from November 10 was assigned to the Regina (1) zone
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 4,437 cases are confirmed
    • 1,232 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 920 cases are from the north area (302 north west, 446 north central, 172 north east)
    • 667 cases are from the Regina area
    • 593 cases are from the south area (247 south west, 248 south central, 98 south east)
    • 554 cases are from the far north area (452 far north west, 17 far north central, 85 far north east)
    • 455 cases are from the central area (203 central west, 252 central east)
    • 16 cases have pending residence information
  • 1,459 cases out of the 4,437 cases are considered active (32.9%)
  • 900 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 9% (401) of cases are travel-related, 45% (2,014) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 22% (974) have no known exposures and 24% (1,048) are under investigation by local public health
  • 127 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 49 individuals are hospitalized; including 36 inpatient hospitalizations and 13 ICU hospitalizations
  • 29 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,949 individuals have recovered (66.5%). There are 15 new recoveries located in the Far North West (1), Far North East (1), North West (3), North Central (6) and North East (4) zones
  • 289,366 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 10, 2020, when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewans per capita rate was 191,842 people tested per million.  The national rate was 268,615 per million population.
