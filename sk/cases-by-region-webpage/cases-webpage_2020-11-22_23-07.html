Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Highlights

  • As of November 22nd, there are 236 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, 236 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 6,473 reported cases.
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (8), Far North Central (1), Far North East (2), North West (17), North Central (24), North East (9), Saskatoon (82), Central West (9), Central East (2), Regina (52), South West (13), South Central (9) and South East (6) zones and two (2) new cases have pending residence information.
  • Three (3) cases with pending residence information (from Nov 14 and Nov 20) were assigned to the Far North West (1), North Central (1) and Regina (1) zones.
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK.
  • 6,473 cases are confirmed:
    • 1,923 cases are from the Saskatoon area;
    • 1,397 cases are from the north area (486 north west, 648 north central, 263 north east);
    • 959 cases are from the Regina area;
    • 866 cases are from the south area (320 south west, 344 south central, 202 south east);
    • 759 cases are from the far north area (531 far north west, 67 far north central, 161 far north east);
    • 541 cases are from the central area (245 central west, 296 central east); and
    • 28 cases have pending residence information.
  • 2,683 cases out of the 6,473 cases are considered active (41.4%).
  • 1,382 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females.
  • 7% (437) of cases are travel-related, 43% (2,800) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 20% (1,315) have no known exposures and 30% (1,921) are under investigation by local public health.
  • 185 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related.
  • A total of 99 individuals are hospitalized; including 80 inpatient hospitalizations and 19 ICU hospitalizations.
  • 33 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.5%.
  • A total of 3,757 individuals have recovered (58.0%). There are 90 new recoveries located in the Far North West (12), North West (6), North East (1), Saskatoon (48), Central West (1), Central East (5), South West (6) and South Central (11) zones.
  • 318,025 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan.
  • As of November 20, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewans per capita rate was 206,493 people tested per million. The national rate was 287,925 people tested per million.
Released: November 22, 2020
