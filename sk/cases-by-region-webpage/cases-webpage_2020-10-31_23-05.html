Renseignements en Franais

Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of October 31st, there are 78 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 78 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province bringing the total to 3,144 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (2), North West (3), North Central (7), North East (6), Saskatoon (41), Central East (1) and Regina (18) zones
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,144 cases are confirmed
    • 802 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 624 cases are from the north area (193 north west, 294 north central, 137 north east)
    • 500 cases are from the south area (229 south west, 215 south central, 56 south east)
    • 440 cases are from the far north area (386 far north west, 0 far north central, 54 far north east)
    • 395 cases are from the Regina area
    • 381 cases are from the central area (196 central west, 185 central east)
    • Two (2) cases have pending residence location
  • 739 cases out of the 3,144 cases are considered active (23.5%)
  • 623 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females
  • 12% (364) of cases are travel-related, 49% (1,546) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 25% (793) have no known exposures and 14% (441) are under investigation by local public health
  • 99 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 29 individuals are hospitalized; including 23 inpatient hospitalizations and six (6) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.8%
  • A total of 2,380 individuals have recovered (75.7%). There are 81 new recoveries located in the Far North West (4), Far North East (4), North West (2), North Central (11), North East (10), Saskatoon (37), Central East (8) and Regina (5) zones.
