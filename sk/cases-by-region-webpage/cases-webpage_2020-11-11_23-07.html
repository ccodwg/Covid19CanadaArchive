Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 11th, there are 112 new confirmed cases of COVID19 112 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province bringing the total to 4,326 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (7), Far North East (8), North West (1), North Central (1), Saskatoon (29), Central East (3), Regina (37), South Central (11) and South East (13) zones and two (2) new cases have pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending residence information from November 8 was assigned to the North Central (1) zone
  • There are no (0) new nonSK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 4,326 cases are confirmed
    • 1,208 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 905 cases are from the north area (295 north west, 438 north central, 172 north east)
    • 641 cases are from the Regina area
    • 577 cases are from the south area (243 south west, 243 south central, 91 south east)
    • 535 cases are from the far north area (447 far north west, 9 far north central, 79 far north east)
    • 448 cases are from the central area (203 central west, 245 central east)
    • 12 cases have pending residence information
  • 1,363 cases out of the 4,326 cases are considered active (31.5%)
  • 875 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, 49% of the cases are males and 51% are females
  • 9% (396) of cases are travelrelated, 45% (1,963) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 22 (960) have no known exposures and 23% (1,007) are under investigation by local public health
  • 124 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 48 individuals are hospitalized; including 37 inpatient hospitalizations and 11 ICU hospitalizations
  • 29 Saskatchewan residents with COVID19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,934 individuals have recovered (67.8%). There are 54 new recoveries located in the Far North West (2), Far North East (2), North West (2), North Central (7), North East (1), Saskatoon (22), Central East (5) and Regina (12) zones and pending location (1)
  • 287,221 COVID19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 9, 2020, when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewan's per capita rate was 191,124 people tested per million population. The national rate was 267,225 people tested per million population.
