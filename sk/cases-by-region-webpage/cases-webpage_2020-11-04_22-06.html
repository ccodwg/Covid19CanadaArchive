Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 4th, there are 37 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 3,408 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (1), North West (1), North Central (1), Saskatoon (13), Central East (6) and Regina (14) zones and one (1) new case has pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending residence information from November 2 has been assigned to the North Central zone
  • Two (2) cases with pending residence information from November 1 were deemed to be out of province residents and removed from the counts
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,408 cases are confirmed
    • 887 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 715 cases are from the north area (222 north west, 350 north central, 143 north east)
    • 504 cases are from the south area (230 south west, 215 south central, 59 south east)
    • 453 cases are from the Regina area
    • 449 cases are from the far north area (394 far north west, 0 far north central, 55 far north east)
    • 397 cases are from the central area (197 central west, 200 central east)
    • Three (3) cases have pending residence information
  • 799 cases out of the 3,408 cases are considered active (23.4%)
  • 689 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 50% of the cases males and 50% females
  • 11% (377) of cases are travel-related, 49% (1,660) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 24% (826) have no known exposures and 16% (545) are under investigation by local public health
  • 107 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 26 individuals are hospitalized; including 22 inpatient hospitalizations and four (4) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,584 individuals have recovered (75.8%). There are 78 new recoveries located in the Far North West (1), Far North East (3), North West (3), North Central (9), North East (17), Saskatoon (22), Regina (17) and South East (6) zones.
Released: November 4, 2020
