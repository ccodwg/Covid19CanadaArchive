Highlights

  • As of November 9th, there are 190 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, 190 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province, bringing the total to 4,087 reported cases.
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (15), Far North Central (1), Far North East (4), North West (19), North Central (15), North East (6), Saskatoon (76), Central West (2), Central East (8), Regina (25), South West (5), South Central (1) and South East (7) zones and six (6) new cases have pending residence information.
  • One (1) case with pending residence information from November 7 was assigned to the North West (1) zone.
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK.
  • 4,087 cases are confirmed.
    • 1,129 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 884 cases are from the north area (283 north west, 431 north central, 170 north east)
    • 573 cases are from the Regina area
    • 547 cases are from the south area (243 south west, 226 south central, 78 south east)
    • 504 cases are from the far north area (434 far north west, 5 far north central, 65 far north east)
    • 437 cases are from the central area (201 central west, 236 central east)
    • 13 cases have pending residence information
  • 1,289 cases out of the 4,087 cases are considered active (31.5%).
  • 832 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases males and 51% females.
  • 9% (388) of cases are travel-related, 46% (1,871) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 22% (887) have no known exposures and 23% (941) are under investigation by local public health.
  • 120 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related.
  • A total of 37 individuals are hospitalized; including 29 inpatient hospitalizations and eight (8) ICU hospitalizations.
  • 29 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%.
  • A total of 2,769 individuals have recovered (67.8%). There are 22 new recoveries located in the North West (5), North Central (7), North East (3), Saskatoon (4) and South Central (3) zones.
Released: November 9, 2020
