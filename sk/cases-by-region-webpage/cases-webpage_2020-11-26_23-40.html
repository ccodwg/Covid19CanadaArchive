Where an official translation is not available, Google Translate can be used. Google Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 26th, there are 299 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, bringing the total to 7,362 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (11), Far North Central (4), Far North East (16), North West (17), North Central (34), North East (3), Saskatoon (125), Central West (4), Central East (6), Regina (44), South West (7), South Central (12) and South East (8) zones and eight (8) new cases have pending residence information
  • One (1) case with pending residence information (from Nov 19) and one (1) case assigned to Saskatoon (from Nov 22) were deemed to be out-of-province residents and removed from the total counts
  • 10 cases with pending resident information (from Nov 5 (1), Nov 11 (1), Nov 20 (1), Nov 21 (1), Nov 22 (3), Nov 23 (1), Nov 24 (2)) were assigned to the North West (3), North Central (4) and Regina (3) zones
  • 18 SK residents previously tested out-of-province (Nov 8 (3), Nov 11 (1), Nov 12 (1), Nov 13 (2), Nov 14 (4), Nov 15 (1), Nov 16 (1), Nov 17 (1), Nov 20 (3), Nov 22 (1)) were assigned to the Far North West (2), North West (10), North Central (1), Saskatoon (4) and Regina (1) zones
  • 7,362 cases are confirmed
    • 2,170 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 1,550 cases are from the north area (542 north west, 729 north central, 279 north east)
    • 1,212 cases are from the Regina area
    • 978 cases are from the south area (356 south west, 383 south central, 239 south east)
    • 848 cases are from the far north area (567 far north west, 79 far north central, 202 far north east)
    • 576 cases are from the central area (262 central west, 314 central east)
    • 28 cases have pending residence information
  • 3,146 cases out of the 7,362 cases are considered active (42.7%)
  • 1,552 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 49% of the cases male and 51% female
  • 6% (463) of cases are travel-related, 45% (3,299) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 20% (1,496) have no known exposures and 29% (2,104) are under investigation by local public health
  • 225 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 108 individuals are hospitalized; including 90 inpatient hospitalizations and 18 ICU hospitalizations
  • 40 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died. Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.5%
  • A total of 4,176 individuals have recovered (56.7%). There are 178 new recoveries located in the Far North West (2), Far North East (2), North West (10), North Central (15), North East (2), Saskatoon (6), Central West (4), Central East (4), Regina (104), South West (6), South Central (7) and South East (16) zones
  • 330,364 COVID-19 tests have been performed in Saskatchewan. As of November 24, 2020 when other provincial and national numbers are available from PHAC, Saskatchewans per capita rate was 212,857 people tested per million population. The national rate was 295,051 people tested per million population.
Released: November 26, 2020
