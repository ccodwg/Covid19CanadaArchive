Where an official translation is not available, Google™ Translate can be used. Google™ Translate is a free online language translation service that can translate text and web pages into different languages. Translations are made available to increase access to Government of Saskatchewan content for populations whose first language is not English.

Software-based translations do not approach the fluency of a native speaker or possess the skill of a professional translator. The translation should not be considered exact, and may include incorrect or offensive language. The Government of Saskatchewan does not warrant the accuracy, reliability or timeliness of any information translated by this system. Some files or items cannot be translated, including graphs, photos and other file formats such as portable document formats (PDFs).

Any person or entities that rely on information obtained from the system does so at his or her own risk. Government of Saskatchewan is not responsible for any damage or issues that may possibly result from using translated website content. Highlights

  • As of November 3rd, there are 81 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 – 81 new cases in Saskatchewan and no (0) new cases are Saskatchewan residents tested out of province – bringing the total to 3,373 reported cases
  • The new cases are located in the Far North West (1), Far North East (1), North West (14), North Central (16), North East (3), Saskatoon (29), Central East (6) and Regina (10) zones and one (1) case has pending residence location
  • Nine (9) cases with pending residence locations reported on November 2 have been assigned to the North West (3), North Central (4), North East (1) and Regina (1) zones
  • There are no (0) new non-SK resident cases that tested positive in SK
  • 3,373 cases are confirmed
    • 874 cases are from the Saskatoon area
    • 712 cases are from the north area (221 north west, 348 north central, 143 north east)
    • 504 cases are from the south area (230 south west, 215 south central, 59 south east)
    • 448 cases are from the far north area (393 far north west, 0 far north central, 55 far north east)
    • 439 cases are from the Regina area
    • 391 cases are from the central area (197 central west, 194 central east)
    • Five (5) cases have pending residence locations
  • 842 cases out of the 3,373 cases are considered active (25.0%)
  • 686 of the cases are individuals in the 19 and under age category; all others are adults, with 50% of the cases males and 50% females
  • 11% (373) of cases are travel-related, 48% (1,627) are contacts to known cases or involved in mass gathering(s), 24% (816) have no known exposures and 17% (557) are under investigation by local public health
  • 105 cases are healthcare workers, although the source of their infections may not be health care related
  • A total of 28 individuals are hospitalized; including 21 inpatient hospitalizations and seven (7) ICU hospitalizations
  • 25 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died; Saskatchewan now has a fatality rate of 0.7%
  • A total of 2,506 individuals have recovered (74.3%). There are 97 new recoveries located in the Far North West (4), Far North East (1), North West (5), North Central (4), North East (1), Saskatoon (51), Central East (12), Regina (16), South West (1) and South East (2) zones.
Released: November 3, 2020
